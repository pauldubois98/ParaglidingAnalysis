Wind NS Simulation вАФ Technical Documentation

Full 3D incompressible Navier-Stokes solver for wind flow over terrain with surface roughness

Overview

The Wind NS simulation solves the full 3D incompressible Navier-Stokes equations to model how a horizontal wind flows over real terrain. Unlike the 2D Shallow Water approach, this solver resolves the complete three-dimensional velocity field вАФ including how wind accelerates over ridges, how it decelerates and recirculates in the lee, and how the logarithmic boundary layer develops above the surface. Surface roughness varies per land cover type, derived from the ESA WorldCover dataset, so forests slow the wind more than bare rock or water.

The simulation uses a Chorin projection method (fractional step) with semi-Lagrangian advection (stable for any CFL number) and a Jacobi pressure Poisson solver. It runs in the browser via a Web Worker on a 3D rectangular grid. Terrain cells are flagged as solid (no-flow); the wind profile is initialised with a logarithmic law anchored to each cell's land-cover roughness length.

Physics Modelled

1. Incompressible Navier-Stokes equations

The flow is governed by the 3D incompressible Navier-Stokes equations. Density is assumed constant (no buoyancy, no temperature). The coordinate system is x = East, y = South, z = Up.

Momentum: вИВU/вИВt + (u¬ЈвИЗ)U = вИТвИВp/вИВx + ќљ¬ЈвИЗ¬≤U вИВV/вИВt + (u¬ЈвИЗ)V = вИТвИВp/вИВy + ќљ¬ЈвИЗ¬≤V вИВW/вИВt + (u¬ЈвИЗ)W = вИТвИВp/вИВz + ќљ¬ЈвИЗ¬≤W Continuity (incompressibility): вИВU/вИВx + вИВV/вИВy + вИВW/вИВz = 0 where u = (U, V, W) is the 3D velocity p is kinematic pressure (Pa / ѕБ = m¬≤/s¬≤) ќљ is kinematic (turbulent) viscosity (m¬≤/s)

There is no gravity term in the momentum equations (the flow is horizontal and density is constant, so buoyancy is absent). The only vertical forcing is through pressure gradients created by the terrain geometry.

2. Logarithmic wind profile

Near a rough surface, the mean wind speed follows a logarithmic profile with height above ground. This is the standard atmospheric surface-layer law:

U(z) = U_ref ¬Ј ln(z / zвВА) / ln(z_ref / zвВА) for z > zвВА U(z) = 0 for z вЙ§ zвВА where z = height above local terrain surface zвВА = aerodynamic roughness length (land-cover dependent) z_ref = reference height where speed = U_ref (typically 100 m) U_ref = reference wind speed (user parameter)

This profile is applied at simulation initialisation and at inflow boundary cells every time step. It ensures the wind is slower close to rough surfaces (forest, built-up areas) and faster over smooth surfaces (water, snow).

The 3D grid is divided into fluid and solid cells. A cell at height z_c above the lowest terrain point is solid if its centre lies below the local terrain surface:

Solid cells are enforced with a no-slip condition: U = V = W = 0. The terrain therefore acts as a physical obstacle that diverts and accelerates the flow.

Numerical Method (Chorin Projection)

Each time step applies the following sequence to advance the velocity and pressure fields:

1

x_d = x вИТ U¬Јdt, y_d = y + V¬Јdt, z_d = z вИТ W¬Јdt
(the +V sign compensates for j increasing southward). This scheme is unconditionally stable for any Courant number, allowing time steps larger than explicit schemes.

3

(the вИТвИВV*/вИВy accounts for the y-axis convention where j increases southward). Non-zero divergence indicates mass imbalance that the pressure step must correct.

Time step calculation

dt_adv = CFL ¬Ј min(dx, dy) / |u|_max [semi-Lagrangian advective limit] dt_diff = diff_safety ¬Ј min(dx¬≤, dy¬≤, dz¬≤) / ќљ [diffusion stability limit] dt = min(dt_adv, dt_diff, dt_max) [smallest wins]

Because semi-Lagrangian advection is unconditionally stable, the time step is often limited by the diffusion condition or by dt_max. The CFL factor can safely exceed 1.0 (typical: 1.5вАУ2.5) without instability from advection.

Simplifications

  • Incompressibility: density is constant everywhere вАФ no sound waves, no compressibility effects. Valid for wind speeds well below ~100 m/s. Eliminates the need for an energy equation.
  • Constant turbulent viscosity ќљ: a single scalar viscosity is used rather than a spatially varying turbulence model (e.g. k-ќµ or Smagorinsky). The Smagorinsky model is used in the Thermal NS simulation but not here вАФ ќљ must be set manually.
  • Neutral atmosphere: no stratification, no buoyancy. The log profile assumes a neutral (adiabatic) atmospheric surface layer. Stable or unstable stratification would modify the profile (Monin-Obukhov similarity theory) but is not implemented.
  • Flat top boundary: the domain top is a horizontal plane with free-slip. This reflects vertical momentum back into the domain rather than allowing it to radiate upward вАФ an artefact for strong updrafts reaching the top.
  • Jacobi pressure solver: converges slowly for large grids; many iterations may be needed to reach acceptable divergence residuals. SOR or a multigrid solver would converge faster but is not implemented.
  • Steady inflow: wind speed and direction are fixed in time. No gusts, no diurnal variation, no mesoscale dynamics.
  • No terrain-following coordinates: the grid is Cartesian; terrain is represented as blocked cells. Cells that straddle the exact terrain surface are either fully solid or fully fluid вАФ no partial-cell treatment. This slightly over-estimates the terrain height by up to one dz.

Limitations

  • No thermal effects вАФ no solar heating, no buoyancy, no convective thermals. For thermally driven vertical motion, use the Thermal NS simulation.
  • No atmospheric stratification вАФ gravity waves, lee waves, and wave breaking (which cause severe turbulence at altitude) require a stratified atmosphere and are absent here.
  • No Coriolis force вАФ negligible for domains < 50 km but formally absent.
  • No moisture вАФ clouds, precipitation, and latent heat are not represented.
  • No synoptic forcing вАФ large-scale pressure gradients, fronts, and mesoscale weather systems are not modelled. The inflow is a simple uniform log profile, not a realistic atmospheric wind profile with jets or wind shear.
  • Slow pressure convergence вАФ the Jacobi solver requires many more iterations than SOR or multigrid to achieve low divergence residuals, especially on fine 3D grids. The flow may not be fully divergence-free within practical iteration counts.
  • No wall model вАФ the log profile is enforced at inflow boundaries and at initialisation, but there is no dynamic wall model that adjusts the near-wall velocity at internal solid faces each step. Flow separation from steep terrain faces may be under-represented.

Tips for Useful Results

  • Allow at least 100вАУ300 time steps for the flow to develop from the initial log profile to a quasi-steady state. The simulation is not initialised with zero velocity, so convergence is faster than a cold start.
  • Increase p_iter to 50вАУ80 if the vertical wind field looks noisy or shows checkerboard patterns вАФ these are symptoms of insufficient pressure convergence (residual divergence).
  • Set ќљ to 5вАУ50 m¬≤/s for mountain-scale flows (dx вЙИ 200 m). At coarser resolution, increase ќљ to damp sub-grid instabilities. At finer resolution, ќљ can be reduced for sharper features.
  • The vertical wind (W) map is most relevant for paragliding: red patches on windward ridges indicate dynamic (orographic) lift zones; blue patches indicate sink on the lee side.
  • The pressure map identifies stagnation zones (high p, blue upwind) and suction peaks (low p over crests). The crest is where orographic lift transitions to lee-side sink.
  • Load ESA WorldCover land cover for per-cell roughness. Without it, a single scalar zвВА is used for the whole domain, which misses the contrast between forest (slow wind aloft, strong drag) and bare rock (fast near-surface wind).
  • Use a higher CFL (1.5вАУ2.0) to speed up the simulation without sacrificing stability. Only reduce CFL if the back-traced departure points land outside the domain frequently (very strong winds with coarse grids).
